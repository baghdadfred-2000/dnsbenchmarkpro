      What is DNS Benchmark Pro?

      DNS Benchmark Pro is a free DNS benchmark and speed test that measures how fast the world's leading public DNS resolvers respond from your connection. Instead of guessing, it runs real DNS-over-HTTPS (DoH) latency tests against 35+ providers — including Cloudflare, Google Public DNS, Quad9, OpenDNS, AdGuard, Mullvad and Control D — then ranks them and recommends the fastest, most reliable DNS for your location, complete with one-click setup steps for Windows and macOS.

      How the DNS speed test works

      When you click Detect Location & Benchmark DNS, the tool sends several timed DoH requests to each provider, discards a warm-up and the slowest outlier, then computes three numbers:

      • • Average latency — the typical round-trip response time in milliseconds. Lower is faster.
      • • Jitter — how much response times vary between requests. Lower means more consistent.
      • • Overall score (out of 100) — a weighted blend of speed (70%) and consistency (30%), normalized across every resolver that responded.

      All testing runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is installed, and your results are not stored on our servers — see our Privacy Policy for details.

      Why your DNS resolver speed matters

      Every time you open a website or app, your device first asks a DNS resolver to translate the domain name into an IP address. A slow or unreliable resolver adds delay before anything else can load — and modern pages trigger dozens of these lookups. Switching to a faster, well-run public DNS can make browsing feel noticeably snappier, improve reliability, and in many cases add privacy or filtering benefits. It won't increase your raw bandwidth, but it removes a hidden bottleneck that affects almost everything you do online.

      Frequently asked questions

      What is the fastest free DNS server?

      It depends on your location and network, which is exactly what this tool measures. In most regions Cloudflare (1.1.1.1), Google (8.8.8.8) and Quad9 (9.9.9.9) rank among the fastest, but the only way to know your fastest is to benchmark from your own connection.

      Is it safe to change my DNS server?

      Yes. Changing DNS only affects how domain names are looked up; it does not expose your data and you can revert to automatic (DHCP) settings at any time. Note your current settings before changing them.

      Does a faster DNS make my internet faster?

      It will not increase your bandwidth, but a faster, more reliable resolver reduces the delay before pages and apps start loading, which makes browsing feel noticeably snappier — especially on sites that pull from many domains.

      What is DNS-over-HTTPS (DoH)?

      DNS-over-HTTPS encrypts DNS queries inside HTTPS so they cannot be read or tampered with on the network. DNS Benchmark Pro uses DoH endpoints to measure each resolver securely and accurately from your browser.

      How accurate is a browser-based DNS benchmark?

      It provides excellent guidance. Browser tests measure real DoH round-trip time from your device, though they are not identical to native system queries and can vary between runs and networks.

      Which DNS is best for privacy?

      Resolvers such as Mullvad, Quad9 and DNS0.eu have strong no-logging or privacy policies. DNS Benchmark Pro labels each resolver's filtering and privacy characteristics so you can balance speed against privacy.
